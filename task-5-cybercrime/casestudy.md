# UPI FRAUD CASE STUDY
UPI fraud is one of the most common forms of cybercrime in India, where attackers manipulate users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking information. 
This type of fraud usually takes place through fake phone calls, SMS messages, or malicious links that appear to come from trusted sources such as banks or digital payment applications.
For example, a college student may receive a call from someone pretending to be a bank representative. 
The caller claims that there is an urgent issue with the student’s bank account and requests immediate verification. 
The student is then asked to share an OTP or approve a payment request on their UPI application. 
Trusting the caller, the student follows the instructions, unknowingly authorizing a transaction that transfers money directly to the fraudster’s account.
In other cases, attackers send fake QR codes or payment links, which, when scanned, lead to unauthorized payments instead of receiving money. 
These scams commonly target students, elderly individuals, and people who are less aware of digital payment security practices.
The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, emotional stress, and misuse of personal and banking information.
With the increasing use of digital payments, it is essential for users to stay alert, verify all requests carefully, and follow safe online practices to protect themselves from such cyber threats.
